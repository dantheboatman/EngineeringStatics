Question 10.6.1.

How are \(k_x\text{,}\) \(k_y\text{,}\) and \(k_o\) related to each other?
Answer.
\begin{equation*} k_x^2 + k_y^2 = k_o^2 \end{equation*}
Solution.
Start with (10.5.2)
\begin{align*} J_O \amp = I_x + I_y \amp \amp \text{divide each term by }A\\ \frac{J_o}{A} \amp = \frac{I_x}{A} + \frac{I_y}{A} \amp \amp \text{apply definitions of }k^2 \\ k_o^2 \amp = k_x^2 + k_y^2 \end{align*}
