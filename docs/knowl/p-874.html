The mixed triple product can be calculated one operation at a time, or in a single step. Either way, the result is a scalar value which may be positive or negative. Both techniques require the components of three vectors
  • \(\hat{\vec{u}}\text{,}\) the unit direction vector of the line or axis of interest. This vector represents the direction of the axis. 1 
  • \(\vec{r}\text{,}\) the position vector from any point on the line of interest to any point on the line of action of the force.
  • \(\vec{F}\text{,}\) the force vector. If you have multiple concurrent forces, you can treat them individually or add them together first and find the moment of the resultant — using Varignon’s principle.
