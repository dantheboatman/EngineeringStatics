The scalar triple product can be calculated efficiently in a single step by evaluating a 3 \(\times\) 3 determinant consisting of the components of \(\hat{\vec{u}}\) in the top row, the components of a position vector \(\vec{r}\) in the middle row, and the components of the \(\vec{F}\) in the bottom row using the augmented determinant method FigureÂ 2.8.6.
\begin{align*} \| \proj_u \vec{M} \| \amp = \hat{\vec{u}} \cdot ( \vec{r}\ \times \vec{F}) \\ \amp = \begin{vmatrix} u_x \amp u_y \amp u_z\\ r_x \amp r_y \amp r_z\\ F_x \amp F_y \amp F_z \end{vmatrix}\\ \amp = (r_y F_z - r_z F_y)\ u_x + (r_z F_x - r_x F_z)\ u_y + (r_x F_y - r_y F_x)\ u_z \end{align*}
