The interactive below compares horizontal and vertical strips for a shape bounded by the parabola \(y^2 = x\) and the diagonal line \(y = x-2\text{.}\) Horizontal strips are a better choice in this case, because the left and right boundaries are easy to express as functions of \(y\text{.}\) If vertical strips are chosen, the parabola must be expressed as two different functions of \(x\text{,}\) and two integrals are needed to cover the area, the first from \(x=0\) to \(x=1\text{,}\) and the second from \(x=1\) to \(x=4\text{.}\)
