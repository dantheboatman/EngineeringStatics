With these vectors known, calculating the moment combines skills you already have learned:
  • finding the moment of a force about a point using the cross product, (4.5.1).
    \begin{equation*} \vec{M} = (\vec{r} \times \vec{F}) \text{.} \end{equation*}
  • finding the scalar projection of one vector onto another vector using the dot product, (2.7.9)
    \begin{equation*} \|\proj_{\vec{u}} \vec{M}\| = \hat{\vec{u}} \cdot \vec{M} \end{equation*}
    This combined dot and cross product is a signed scalar value called the scalar triple product. A positive sign indicates that the moment vector points in the positive \(\hat{\vec{u}}\) direction.
  • and multiplying a scalar projection by a unit vector to find the vector projection, (2.7.10)
    \begin{equation} \vec{M}_{\hat{\vec{u}}} = \|\proj_{\vec{u}} \vec{M}\|\ \hat{\vec{u}}\text{.}\tag{4.5.6} \end{equation}
