    Section 2.1 1d-definitions.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Adjust the magnitude and direction of the vector with the dot at the tip, and adjust the point of application by dragging the line of action and sliding the tail along it.

    Section 2.2 1d-addition.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    The interactive shows \(\vec{A}+ \vec{B}= \vec{R}.\)
    You can adjust the magnitude and direction of vectors with the tips and their position along the line of action with the tails. When they arranged tip-to-tail, the resultant vector will appear. The vectors are represented as scalar components multiplied by unit vector \(\ihat\text{.}\)
    This is just a graphic representation of tip-to-tail addition; \(2\ihat + 3 \ihat = 5 \ihat\) regardless of where \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) are located on the line of action.

    Section 2.3 rectangular-coordinates.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Move the dot to change the rectangular (\(x\) and \(y\)) coordinates of \(P\text{.}\)

    Section 2.4 polar-coordinates.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Move the dot to change the polar coordinates, (\(r\) and \(\theta\)) of \(P\text{.}\)

    Section 2.5 r-to-p-to-r.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    \(P\) is defined by its rectangular components \((x,y)\) and/or its polar coordinates \((r;\theta)\text{.}\)
    Move the dot to change them simultaneously. They are related by right triangle trigonometry.

    Section 2.6 rectangular-coordinates-3d.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Move the red point to move the vector in space. Click the red point to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode.
    The vector can be visualized as the diagonal of a rectangular box with the \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) components as the side lengths.

    Section 2.7 direction-cosines-3d.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Move the red point to move the vector in space. Click the red point to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Rotate the diagram to see it from different angles.
    Note that the red, blue and green triangles are right angled although it is not always easy to see this. The vector is the hypotenuse and the adjacent sides are the \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) and \(z\) rectangular components. The rectangular components and the direction angles are shown.

    Section 2.8 spherical-coordinates.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Use the red point to move the tip of the vector anywhere on the spherical surface. The rectangular and spherical coordinates are shown.

    Section 2.9 cylindrical-coordinates.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    Use the red point to move the tip of the vector along the cylindrical surface. The rectangular and cylindrical coordinates are shown.

    Section 2.10 unit_vector.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows the graphical expression and component values of force \(\vec{F}\) and its unit vector \(\hat{\mathbf{F}}\text{.}\)

    Section 2.11 2d-addition-views.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This diagram demonstrates vector addition using the triangle and parallelogram rules. Change the slider to see the different interpretations.
    Note that polar coordinates are a better fit for graphing magnitude and direction, while Cartesian coordinates are better for rectangular components.

    Section 2.12 orthogonal-components.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    In this interactive you can rotate the vector \(\vec{F}\) and also rotate the coordinate system and see the components of \(\vec{F}\) in the \(x\)-\(y\) and \(x'\)-\(y'\) directions.

    Section 2.13 rect_components_add.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive represents vector addition \(\vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B} + \vec{C}\text{.}\)
    You can change the magnitude and direction of the three components, and view the resultant. You can also see the rectangular components of the three component vectors.

    Section 2.14 3d-addition.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows the vector sum of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\text{.}\)
    You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red dots. Click dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Coordinates of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table.

    Section 2.15 2d-dot-product.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive demonstrates the relationship between vectors \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) and the projection of \(\vec{B}\) onto \(\vec{A}\text{.}\) The checkbox switches between showing \(\proj_\vec{A}\vec{B}\) and \(\proj_\vec{B}\vec{A}\text{.}\)

    Section 2.16 3d-dot_product.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows the vector projection of \(\vec{A}\) on \(\vec{B}\) or of \(\vec{B}\) on \(\vec{A}\text{.}\)
    You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red dots or entering values into the table cells. Click dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. The components of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table.

    Section 2.17 3d-direction-vector.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows \(\vec{r}_{AB}\text{,}\) the displacement vector from \(\vec{A}\) to \(\vec{B}\) and the corresponding unit displacement vector \(\lambda_{AB}\text{.}\)
    You may change \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) by moving the red and blue dots. Click the dot to switch between \(x\)-\(y\) mode and \(z\) mode. Coordinates of \(\vec{A}\) and \(\vec{B}\) can also be entered into the table directly.

    Section 2.18 cross-product.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows the relation of the cross product to the two vectors and the angle between them.

    Section 2.19 unit_circle.ggb

    Interactive Diagram.

    This interactive shows the graphical relations between the unit vector \(\hat{\vec{F}}\) and the unit circle. \(\ihat\) and \(\jhat\) are the standard unit vectors in the \(x\) and \(y\) directions.
