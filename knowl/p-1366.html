The defining equations for centroids are similar to the equations for Centers of GravityÂ (7.2.2) but with volume used as the weighting factor for three-dimensional shapes
\begin{equation} \bar{x}=\frac{\sum \bar{x}_{i} V_i}{\sum V_i} \quad \bar{y}=\frac{\sum \bar{y}_{i} V_i}{\sum V_i} \quad \bar{z}=\frac{\sum \bar{z}_{i} V_i}{\sum V_i} \text{,}\tag{7.4.1} \end{equation}
and area for two-dimensional shapes
\begin{equation} \bar{x}=\frac{\sum \bar{x}_{i} A_i}{\sum A_i} \quad \bar{y}=\frac{\sum \bar{y}_{i} A_i}{\sum A_i} \text{.}\tag{7.4.2} \end{equation}
