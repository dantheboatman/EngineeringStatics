Example 2.8.8. 3D Cross Product.

Find the cross product of \(\vec{A} = \m{\langle 2,4,-1 \rangle} \) and \(\vec{B} = \N{\langle 10, 25, 20 \rangle}\text{.}\)
Here, we are crossing a distance \(\vec{A}\) and with a force \(\vec{B}.\) This calculation is equivalent to finding the moment about a point \(P\) caused by force \(\vec{B}\) acting distance \(\vec{A}\) from \(P\text{.}\) You will learn about moments in Chapter 4.
Answer.
\begin{gather*} \vec{A} \times \vec{B} = \Nm{\langle 105, -50, 10 \rangle} \end{gather*}
Solution 1.
To solve, set up the augmented determinant and evaluate it by adding the left-to-right diagonals and subtracting the right-to-left diagonals using equation (2.8.6).
\begin{align*} \vec{A} \times \vec{B} \amp= \begin{vmatrix} \ihat \amp \jhat \amp \khat \\ 2 \amp 4 \amp -1 \\ 10 \amp 25 \amp 20 \end{vmatrix} \begin{matrix} \ihat \amp \jhat\\ 2 \amp 4\\ 10 \amp 25 \end{matrix} \\ \amp = (4 \cdot 20)\;\ihat + (-1 \cdot 10)\;\jhat + (2 \cdot 25) \;\khat - (4 \cdot 10) \;\khat -(-1 \cdot 25) \;\ihat - (2 \cdot 20) \;\jhat \\ \amp = (80 + 25) \;\ihat + (-10 - 40) \;\jhat + (50 - 40) \;\khat \\ \amp = \Nm{\langle 105, -50, 10 \rangle} \end{align*}
Thus, the force \(\vec{B}\) creates a three-dimensional rotational moment equal to \(\Nm{\langle 105, -50, 10 \rangle}.\)
