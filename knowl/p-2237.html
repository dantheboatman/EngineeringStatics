For vertical strips, which are parallel to the \(y\) axis we can use the definition of the Moment of Inertia.
\begin{align*} I_y \amp = \int x^2 dA\\ \amp = \int_0^{0.5} {x^2} \left ( \frac{x}{4} - \frac{x^2}{2} \right ) dx\\ \amp= \int_0^{1/2} \left( \frac{x^3}{4} - \frac{x^4}{2} \right) dx \\ \amp= \left . \left( \frac{x^4}{16} - \frac{x^5}{12} \right )\right \vert_0^{1/2}\\ \amp= \left( \frac{({1/2})^4}{16} - \frac{{(1/2)}^5}{10} \right ) \\ \amp = \frac{1}{64} \left( \frac{1}{4}- \frac{1}{5} \right)\\ I_y \amp = \frac{1}{1280} = \cm{7.81 \times 10^{-4}}^4 \end{align*}
