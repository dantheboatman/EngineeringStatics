Inserting \(dy\ dx\) for \(dA\) and the limits into (10.1.3), and integrating gives
\begin{align*} I_x \amp = \int_A y^2\ dA \\ \amp = \int_0^b \int_0^h y^2 \ dy \ dx\\ \amp = \int_0^b \left . \frac{y^3}{3} \ dy \right \vert_0^h \ dx\\ \amp = \int_0^b \boxed{\frac{h^3}{3}\ dx} \\ \amp = \frac{h^3}{3} \int_0^b \ dx \\ I_x \amp = \frac{bh^3}{3}\text{.} \end{align*}
