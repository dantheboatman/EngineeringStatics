You have three \(\ft{24}\) long wooden 2\(\times\)6’s and you want to nail them together them to make the stiffest possible beam. The stiffness of a beam is proportional to the moment of inertia of the beam’s cross-section about a horizontal axis passing through its centroid. The actual dimensions of nominal 2\(\times\)6 lumber are \(\inch{1.5}\) by \(\inch{5.5}\text{.}\)
