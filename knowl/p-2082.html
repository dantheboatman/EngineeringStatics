FigureÂ 9.6.1 shows a journal bearing supporting a shaft with a vertical load \(P\text{.}\) Initially the contact point is located directly below the load along its line of action. When a clockwise moment \(M\) is applied to rotate the shaft, friction between the shaft and bearing causes the surfaces to stick together, and the shaft climbs up the bearing surface until impending motion is reached and slipping occurs. Similarly, when a CCW moment \(M\) is applied, the contact point will shift to the left.
