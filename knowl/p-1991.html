Left-handed screws are less common but are found in some applications, especially:
  • machines where reverse-threading prevents them from loosening gradually under the torque of the moving part. For example, the left pedal on a bicycle.
  • machines where the movement of the screw creates dual motion, like a hand-screw wood clamp.
  • situations where you do not want to mix up constituents. Cutting torches use right-hand thread for the oxygen and left-hand threads for the acetylene connections.
