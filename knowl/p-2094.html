The coefficient of friction and pressure terms are constant so can be moved outside the integral, and since pressure is defined as force per unit are area \(p = F/A\text{,}\) the pressure term can be replaced with the applied load divided by the bearing contact area,
\begin{equation*} p = \frac{P}{\pi (r_o^2 -r_i^2)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
