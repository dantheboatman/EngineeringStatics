Friction has two distinct regions as shown in FigureÂ 9.1.6, and the value of \(\mu\) is different in each region. The region from point one to point two, where the force of friction increases linearly with load is called the static friction region. Here you must use the coefficient of static friction \(\mu_s\text{.}\) The region from point two to point three, where the friction remains roughly constant is called the kinetic friction region. In this region you must use the coefficient of kinetic friction \(\mu_k\text{.}\)
