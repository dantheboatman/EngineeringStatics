And summing forces in the \(y\) direction gives
\begin{align*} \sum F_y \amp = 0\\ dN \amp = T \sin (d\theta/2) + (T+dT) \sin (d\theta/2)\\ dN \amp = (2T + dT) \sin (d\theta/2)\\ \amp \approx 2T (d\theta/2) + dT (d\theta/2) \end{align*}
where we have used the small angle approximation \(\sin(d\theta/2) \approx d\theta/2\text{.}\) Dropping the second order differential term \(dT\; d\theta\) as negligible, yields
\begin{equation*} dN = T d\theta\text{.} \end{equation*}
